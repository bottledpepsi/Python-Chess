How to play

From opening the app to finishing your first game.

Starting a game

When you open Python Chess, the home screen gives you a few ways to jump in:

  • Play a friend. Two people take turns on the same computer. The board flips around automatically between turns so whoever's playing always sees it from their own side.
  • Play the computer. You against the built-in computer opponent. Pick a difficulty before you start.
  • Engine match. Sit back and watch the computer play itself. Good for testing things out, or just watching a game happen.
  • Preferences. Change settings before you play. More on that in Settings & customization.

Moving pieces

You can move pieces two ways, whichever feels more natural to you:

  • Click to move. Click a piece to select it, then click the square you want to move it to.
  • Drag and drop. Click and hold a piece, drag it over, and let go.

Squares you can legally move to get marked with a dot. If a move would capture something, you'll see a ring around that square instead. The square a piece moved from and to stays highlighted, and if your king's in check, that square lights up too.

Promoting a pawn

When a pawn makes it to the far end of the board, a little strip pops up so you can pick what it turns into. Click the piece you want, or just press Q, R, B, or N on your keyboard for queen, rook, bishop, or knight.

Drawing arrows

Click and drag with the right mouse button to draw an arrow on the board. Handy for planning a move out loud, or showing the person you're playing what you're thinking. Left-click anywhere on the board and your arrows clear.

During the game

A few buttons stick around while you're playing:

  • Menu. Save and quit, or duck into Preferences without losing your spot.
  • Analysis. Turns on live move evaluation, see Features: Analysis mode.
  • Resign and Offer Draw. Both show up in two-player and computer games. Neither can be undone, so you'll get asked to confirm first.
  • Export PGN. Saves the current game to a file. Details in Saves & exporting games.

Reviewing a finished game

Once a game ends, you'll see the result along with two options. Rematch starts a fresh game with the same settings, and Review Game lets you step back through every move that was played. While reviewing, use the arrow keys or click any move in the history panel to jump straight there.

Keyboard shortcuts

→Step forward through move history
