b1 · Using the app

Features

A tour of everything Python Chess can do.

Game modes

01

Player vs Player

Two people play on the same screen, taking turns. The board rotates smoothly between moves, so whoever's up always sees the board from their own side of the table.

02

Player vs Computer

Play against the built-in computer opponent. A 10-level difficulty slider runs from relaxed and forgiving all the way up to genuinely tough, with a short description at every level so you roughly know what you're picking.

Stockfish, an optional stronger opponent

Want an opponent that scales well past the built-in one? Switch over to Stockfish, one of the strongest chess engines out there. Instead of the 10-level slider, Stockfish uses a rating slider (1320 to 3190) so you can dial in a pretty specific level of challenge.

You don't need to install anything separately for this. The app can download and set up the right version of Stockfish for your computer automatically, right from the preferences screen.

Analysis mode

Turn on analysis with the in-game button to see how strong the current position actually is, either while you're playing or while reviewing a finished game. It's powered by Stockfish, so you'll need that set up first.

  • An evaluation bar shows which side is doing better, and roughly by how much, animating smoothly instead of jumping around.
  • Best-move arrows point out the strongest move found in the current position.

If Stockfish isn't set up yet, analysis just won't be available, and you'll get a one-time message explaining why instead of things quietly failing.

Chess clock

Add a time limit to a two-player game. Before things start, pick from a few common time controls:

PresetWhat it means
NoneNo clock, play at your own pace
1+01 minute per player, no bonus time
3+23 minutes per player, plus 2 seconds added after each move
5+05 minutes per player, no bonus time
10+010 minutes per player, no bonus time
15+1015 minutes per player, plus 10 seconds added after each move

Both clocks sit above the board and switch automatically after each move. Running low turns your clock a warning color, and hitting zero means you lose on time. Clocks only apply to two-player games, computer games never get timed.

If you close the app mid-game with the clock running, your remaining time gets saved exactly as it was. Time spent away from the app never counts against either player.

Board and interface

  • Move it your way. Click to select and move, or just drag and drop.
  • Smooth animation. Pieces glide into place, and the board itself animates whenever it flips between turns.
  • Clear move indicators. Dots for open squares, rings for captures, plus highlights for the last move, the selected square, and check.
  • Captured piece trays show what's been taken and who's ahead on material.
  • Move history panel. Scroll through it, or click any move to jump straight to that point.
  • Arrow drawing. Right-click and drag on the board to sketch out an idea.
  • Hover over anything clickable and you'll get a hand cursor, so it's always obvious what you can interact with.

Review mode

Step through any game move by move, forward or backward, using the arrow keys or by clicking a move in the history panel. Each step animates smoothly, so it's easy to keep track of where you are.

Sound

Sound effects play for moves, captures, check, checkmate, and draws. You can mute everything from Preferences whenever you want.

Saving your progress

Games save themselves automatically as you play, so closing the app and picking up right where you left off is never an issue. More on this in Saves & exporting games.

Exporting games

Export the game you're playing to a standard .pgn file whenever you like, so you can share it or open it in other chess software. See Saves & exporting games: exporting to PGN.

Accessibility

  • Full keyboard control. Every menu and button can be reached and used without a mouse.
  • High-contrast text throughout the interface, meeting WCAG-AA guidelines.
  • Fullscreen toggle with F11.
  • Reduced motion option, for anyone who'd rather skip piece animation, board flipping, and fade effects.
  • A colorblind-friendly board theme is available too, see Settings & customization.
