Frequently asked questions

Is Python Chess free?

Yep. It's free, open source, and licensed under GPL-3.0. No ads, no accounts, no in-app purchases.

Do I need an internet connection to play?

Nope, Python Chess runs entirely on your computer. The only time it reaches out to the internet is if you choose to download the optional Stockfish engine for a stronger opponent or for analysis.

Can I play online against someone else?

Not right now. Python Chess is built for two people on the same computer, or for playing against the built-in computer opponent.

What's the difference between the built-in opponent and Stockfish?

The built-in opponent works right away with a simple 10-level difficulty picker. Stockfish is a separate, optional engine you can add for a much wider and more precise range of strength (using a rating slider), and it's also what powers the live analysis feature. See Features: Stockfish.

Will I lose my game if I close the app?

No, games save automatically as you play and they'll be there waiting when you reopen the app. See Saves & exporting games.

Can I take back a move?

Not during play, moves are final once made, same as a real game would be. You can always review a finished game move by move afterward though, and export it to PGN to study later.

Does it work on my operating system?

Yes. Python Chess has ready-to-run builds for Windows, macOS, and Linux. See Installation.

I found a bug, or I have a suggestion. Where do I report it?

Please open an issue on the project's GitHub page.
