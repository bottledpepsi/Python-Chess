b3 · Using the app

Saves & exporting games

Your progress saves itself. You can also export any game to share it or open it somewhere else.

Autosave

Games against a friend or against the computer save automatically as you play. Close the app mid-game and just reopen it later, your game will be right where you left it, board position, move history, and the chess clock too if you're using one.

Saves survive a computer restart, and there's some care put into never losing one. If the app were to crash mid-save, your previous save stays intact instead of getting left half-written.

Where saves live

Saves sit in a per-user application data folder on your computer. You don't need to manage this yourself, but if you're curious where it actually is, check Troubleshooting: finding your files.

Exporting to PGN

PGN (Portable Game Notation) is the standard file format for chess games, most chess software and websites can open it. To export your current game, open the in-game menu and pick Export PGN.

  • Each export gets its own timestamped file, so exporting the same game more than once won't overwrite an earlier copy.
  • Exported files include the date, the result, and, for games against the computer, the difficulty level you played at.

See Troubleshooting: finding your files for exactly where exported files end up on your system.
